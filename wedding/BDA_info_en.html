Bermuda Information

Background

 

    Bermuda is a group of islands formed by long-extinct volcanoes in the Atlantic Ocean .  Bermuda has a humid subtropical climate, with average temperatures in July 29*C/ 84*F (day), and 24*C/75*F (night). The main island is 35 km/ 22 miles long, and 1.6 km/ 1 mile wide (tiny!).  Due to the small size, although hurricane season is from June - November, we rarely get a direct hit.  Bermuda is a British Overseas Territory.  Did you know that a Spaniard (Juan de Bermudez) was the navigator who discovered these islands in 1503.

 

 US, Canadian and European citizens do not need a visa for Bermuda.

    

Currency
 

We use colourful Bermuda dollars (BMD), but US dollars are also legal tender and you won’t have any trouble using them here.  US dollars have a fixed exchange rate of $1USD=$1BMD making it very easy.  So the best is to bring US dollars for small things, and use your card for anything else, as they are all widely accepted (Mastercard, Visa, AmEx).

 

Getting there
 

    - From Europe -  

British Airways is the only carrier from Europe (London, England- 6.5 hour flight direct).  You can either travel via British Airways, or any transatlantic carrier to Eastern USA or Canada (New York, Boston, Florida, Atlanta, Toronto) and connect to a short flight onwards (2.5 hours) to Bermuda.

 

    - From Eastern USA and Canada -  

There are many carriers- US Airways, Delta, Air Canada, American Airlines, Jet Blue.

 

Check search engines like skyscanner.net, kayak.com, hipmunk.com to find the best options. Make sure though to book through a reputable online travel agency, or book directly with the airline.

 

Accomodation
 

In Bermuda, guest accommodation tends to be on the expensive side.  Due to the sheer beauty and tranquility of Bermuda, there is an influx of tourists in the summer, so please ensure you make your reservations as quickly as possible.

 

Hotels

There are several very good hotels. We recommend the Fairmont Southampton . It has it’s own private beach and impeccable service.  If you find another hotel and want our advice, just email us. We’d be happy to help.

 

Bed and Breakfasts/ Private home rental

A more economical option than a hotel would be to search the private guest houses and home rentals. Some useful websites: airbnb.com, homeaway.com, bermudarentals.com.

 

Glamping (glamorous camping)

For the slightly more adventurous, we have rented Paget Island, which has buildings of dorm bunks with air conditioning, lots of well kept toilets and hot showers, a large kitchen and BBQ, as well as a private beach, kayaks to rent, an adventure course and a large lawn to play football and frisbee and relax.  Please feel free to join us on the island, just send us an email so we can confirm your place. The cost will be $10/per night- a bargain =)

 

Transportation
 

We drive on the LEFT (like in England).  The island wide speed limit is 35km/h (20miles/h). You really feel like time slows down when you get here =)

 

- Car/ Moped -    You cannot rent a car in Bermuda, but you can rent a MOPED.

Cost- starts at $55 for one day, and cost/day goes down the longer you rent. Moped rental  will come with helmets and an introductory lesson to make sure you feel safe. [http://www.oleandercycles.bm/] or check hotels as they often do rentals too.

 

- Bus - convenient and inexpensive. Some routes do not run in the evening.
